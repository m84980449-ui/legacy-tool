How Position Sizing Works

The fundamental formula is:

  • Risk Amount = Account Balance × Risk %
  • Stop Distance = |Entry - Stop Loss|
  • Position Size = Risk Amount ÷ Stop Distance
  • Margin = Position Size × Entry Price ÷ Leverage

Example: $1,000 account, 1% risk, entry $67,000, stop $65,000 → Risk = $10, Stop Distance = $2,000, Position = 0.005 BTC. With 5x leverage, you only need $67 margin.

Why it matters: Leverage doesn't change your position size — it only reduces the margin needed. Never size based on leverage, always size based on your stop-loss.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the ideal risk per trade?

Professional traders typically risk 1–2% per trade. This ensures you can survive 20–50 consecutive losses without blowing your account.

Does leverage affect my position size?

No. Leverage only affects the margin (collateral) required. Your position size is determined by your risk and stop-loss distance, not your leverage.

What's a good risk/reward ratio?

A minimum of 1:2 (risk $1 to make $2). Most professionals aim for 1:3 or higher. Below 1:1.5, the math doesn't work long-term even with a high win rate.
